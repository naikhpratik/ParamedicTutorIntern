2. Elderly Fall

You are called emergent to a 86 year old male patient who recently fell from standing. The patient is at a nursing home and staff state that they do not perform CPR at this facility due to liability. You find the patient unconscious, unresponsive, not breathing.
  • In this scenario it is best to first:

    1. Move the patient to the best location
    2. Prepare for intubation
    3. Start CPR
    4. Check to see if the patient has a current DNR

  • The correct answer is 4. Check to see if the patient has a current DNR.

    Because this patient has been found in a nursing home, it is best to check for a DNR. If no DNR is presented or known, begin CPR.

· After 2 minutes of compressions and ventilations you see this on the cardiac monitor.

    • What is the best treatment option for this rhythm strip?

      1. Continue CPR and administer epinephrine 1:10000
      2. Defibrillate 200J
      3. Synchronize cardiovert
      4. Start cardiac pacing

    • The correct answer is 2. Defibrillate 200J.

      This rhythm is ventricular fibrillation. Defibrillation is required and dosage is per manufacturer’s recommendation or 200J per AHA.

    • You defibrillate the patient at 200J, immediately following defibrillation you should:

      1. Continue CPR
      2. Check monitor for a pulse
      3. Feel for a pulse
      4. Administer epinephrine 1:10000

    • The correct answer is 1. Continue CPR.

      No matter the result of defibrillation, it is now the standard to “support” the heart by performing 2 more minutes of CPR and checking for pulse at next rhythm check.

    • During the period when this patient is not intubated, and CPR is in progress, the appropriate compression to ventilation ratio for an adult patient is:

      1. every 6-8 seconds
      2. every 3-5 seconds
      3. 30:2
      4. 15:2

    • The correct answer is 3. 30:2.

      With a BVM or other basic airway adjunct, the compression to ventilation ratio should be 30:2 for adult patients.

    · Later on in the cardiac arrest, you have administered 1mg epinephrine 1:10, amiodarone 300mg, 1mg epinephrine 1:10, amiodarone 150mg, and the patient remains in V-Fib despite defibrillation every 2 minutes.
    • The next medication in this scenario is:

      1. 1mg epinephrine 1:1
      2. 1mg epinephrine 1:10000
      3. Amiodarone 150mg
      4. Lidocaine 1-1.5 mg kg

    • The correct answer is 2. 1mg epinephrine 1:10000.

      Per ACLS algorithm, this patient should receive another dose of epinephrine 1:10.

    • This patient remains a V-fib arrest despite all efforts. At this point in the resuscitation it is important to consider:

      1. Reversible causes
      2. Termination of resuscitation
      3. Contact medical control for direction
      4. Transport emergent to the nearest facility

    • The correct answer is 1. Reversible causes.

      Treatment of reversible causes is always a factor, but in this case it is very important. Termination of resuscitation is not recommended unless patient converts to asystole, but will be based on local protocol.
