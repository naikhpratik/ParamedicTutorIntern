7. Ill Elderly

Your patient is 90 year old female patient who went to the doctor because she is not feeling well. The patient is AAOx4 at this time. The local clinic called you and shows you this EKG:

    • What would you call this strip?

      1. 2nd degree heart block type 1
      2. 2nd degree heart block type 2
      3. 3rd degree heart block
      4. SA node exit block

    • The correct answer is 3. 3rd degree heart block.

      The atria are working well based on the P waves marching through the strip, but they are not talking to the ventricles. This is the definition of 3rd degree heart block, itâ€™s AV dissociation.

    • What treatment plan is best if this patient is hypotensive?

      1. Atropine
      2. Dopamine
      3. Epinephrine
      4. Pacing

    • The correct answer is 4. Pacing.

      Atropine is unlikely to work because atropine has the most effect on the atria, and in this patient the atria are not talking to the ventricles. Dopamine and epinephrine would work but pacing is probably the best option for this patient, at this time.

    • What treatment plan is best if this patient is normo-tensive?

      1. Place defibrillation patches on patient and monitor
      2. Pace
      3. Synchronized cardioversion
      4. Atropine

    • The correct answer is 1. Place defibrillation patches on patient and monitor.

      If the patient is normotensive then they are compensating well for their heart condition. Preparing for pacing, monitoring, and establishing an IV are good ideas on this patient.
