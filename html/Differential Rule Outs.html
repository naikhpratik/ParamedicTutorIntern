Differential Rule Outs and Considerations

  • Acute Coronary Syndromes (Heart Attacks)
    • A full OPQRST
    • Current symptoms: diaphoresis, nausea, vomiting, shortness of breath, indigestion, palpitations, dizziness, weakness or syncope
    • Past history
    • Vitals: BP, pulse, mental status, skin color, temperature
    • Rhythm strip? ASA given? Erectile dysfunction drugs?
    • AED, IV, O2, ASA, fluids

  • Cardiac Arrest/Resuscitation
    • Down time?
    • Surroundings: drugs, lightning, crime scene, trauma
    • Time CPR started and by whom?
    • Advisory of shock or rhythm interpreted and shocks given
    • Interventions: airway, IV - fluid, shocks, meds
    • How patient was found
    • Pupils
    • Pallor, cyanosis or lividity, lines of demarcation
    • DNR – advanced directives
    • Interventions: AED, O2, BVM, IV, CPR

  • Congestive Heart Failure
    • Differential Rule Outs: heart attack, asthma, COPD
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: right sided = fluid in the feet/left sided = fluids in the lungs - JVD
      • Respiratory: mechanical blockage in lungs vs. physiological?
    • Interventions: Lasix/Morphine/Nitro/Oxygen - CPAP, BVM, O2, IV
      • Current meds: compliant?
      • Chest pain/weakness/syncope?
      • Vials: tachycarida? hypo/hypertensive? tachypnea?
      • Other assessments: breath sounds, pulse Ox, capnography, 12 Lead

  • Abdominal Pain
    • Differential rule outs: heart attack, sepsis, UTI, overdose, AAA, appendicitis
    • System Considerations:
      • GI: blockage, bleeding, infection
      • Reproductive/Urinary: female - ectopic pregnancy, UTI, obstruction, bleeding, infection
      • Cardiovascular: AAA, heart attack
      • Digestive: flu, constipation, trauma
    • Interventions: IV, O2, pain medications

  • Allergies and Anaphylaxis
    • Differential rule outs:
      • Swelling/inflammation: asthma, epiglottitis
      • Infection: pneumonia, croup
      • Trauma: foreign body, burns, inhalations, penetrating trauma
      • Other: anxiety, PE
    • System Considerations:
      • Respiratory: Asthma, PE
      • Integumentry: localized rash or allergy
      • Immunosuppressant: known antigen, known exposure and route of exposure
    • Interventions: IV, O2, Benadryl, Epi

  • Altered Mental Status
    • Differential rule outs: alcohol, drugs, diabetes, overdose, CVA
    • System Considerations:
      • Endocrine: blood sugar, electrolytes
      • Cardiovascular: MI, vascular obstruction, brain attack
      • Respiratory: hypoxia due to respiratory infection or blockage
      • GI: bleed = hypovolemia, infection
      • Urinary: UTI, fever
      • Musculoskeletal: trauma
      • Nervous: meds/drugs
    • Interventions: D50, Glucose, O2, IV

  • Behavioral and Psychiatric Disorders
    • Differential rule outs: alcohol, drugs, diabetes, overdose, CVA, trauma
    • System Considerations:
      • Endocrine: diabetes
      • Respiratory: infection
      • Cardiovascular: drugs and alcohol, non-compliant with meds, CVA
      • Urinary: infection
      • Nervous: drugs, alcohol
      • Musculoskeletal: trauma
    • History of Present Illness: suicidal? hallucinations? drugs/alcohol?
    • Assessments: pupils? breath? medical alert tags? full mental status assessment? restraints?
    • People involved in restraint, and type of restraints used
    • Rule out: HEAD INJURY, LOW BLOOD SUGAR

  • CVA - Stroke
    • Differential rule outs: alcohol, drugs, diabetes, trauma, infection, fever
    • System Considerations:
      • Endocrine: blood sugar
      • Endocrine: diabetes
      • Reparatory: pneumonia
      • Cardiovascular: bleed in the brain, clot in the brain
      • GI: alcohol, drugs, OD
      • Urinary: UTI
    • IMPORTANT: Onset of symptoms, previous TIA’s or CVA’s
    • Symptoms: nausea, dizziness, slurred speech, altered sensation etc.
    • Hypertension and bradycardia? = increased ICP
    • Bells Palsy? Cannot raise eyebrows, then maybe Bell’s
    • Interventions: O2, IV, rapid transport

  • Dyspnea - Respiratory Distress
    • Differential rule outs: anaphylaxis, asthma, COPD, CHF, epiglottitis, pneumothorax, pneumonia, pulmonary emboli
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: heart attack, PE
      • Respiratory: asthma, COPD, bronchitis, emphysema, pneumonia, trauma
      • Endocrine: hyperglycemia
      • Immunosuppressent: anaphylaxis
    • Interventions: O2, IV, CPAP, nebulizer, albuterol, atrovent, patient assist

  • Hypoglycemia and Hyperglycemia
    • Differential rule outs: trauma, CVA
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: brain attack
      • Respiratory: infection
      • Endocrine: hypo/hyperglycemia
      • Nervous: Seizures?
      • Reproductive: Female = pregnant?
    • Interventions: IV, D50, Glucose, Fluid
    • Presence of Seizures?

  • Hypotension - Shock
    • Differential rule outs: Types of shock: anaphylaxis, cardiogenic, hypovolemic, neurogenic, septic
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: cardiogenic, hypovolemic
      • Nervous: neurogenic
      • GI/UI: septic
    • Previous events: trauma, allergies, medical conditions
    • Overt symptoms: syncope, weakness, dizziness, thirst
    • Stage of shock: tachycardia with normal BP = early
    • Stage of shock: tachycardia with low BP = late
    • Skin tone
    • Interventions: Specific to type of shock i.e. cardiogenic, anaphylactic

  • Seizures
    • Differential rule outs: past seizures, trauma, CVA, pregnancy
    • System Considerations:
      • Nervous: seizure history
      • Cardiovascular: trauma, CVA
      • Reproductive: female = pregnant?
    • LOC: combatitive, disoriented, unconscious
    • Meds: compliant?
    • Trauma: before, during, or after seizure. Mouth/Tongue/Spine/Head
    • Hypoglycemia?
    • Interventions: glucose, O2, airway control
    • First seizure?

  • Syncope
    • Differential rule outs: heart attack, CVA, hypoglycemia, AAA
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: syncope, heart attack, dangerous rhythm
      • GI: bleeding somewhere? recent vomiting/fluid loss
    • History of Present Illness: Activity just prior. Only dizzy when standing up, or also when lying down?
    • Associated symptoms: dizziness, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, bleeding, headache?

  • Poisoning and Intoxicants
    • Differential rule outs: OD, accidental poisoning, suicidal patient, drug attic, diabetic, CVA, trauma
    • Possible OD’s: alcohol, antabuse, aspirin, barbiturates, Benadryl, beta blockers, benzodiazapines, CO, cocaine, caustics, MDMA (ecstasy), GHB, hallucinogenics, ketamine, opiates and narcotics, tricyclic antidepressants
    • System Considerations:
      • GI: burns to GI tract
      • Respiratory: burns to airway
      • Nervous: seizures, nervous system depression
      • Cardiovascular: heart attack, dangerous rhythm, alteration of cardiac rate
    • Interventions: poison dependent, ventilation will be important

  • Trauma
    • Mechanism? Up and over?, Down and under?
    • Mechanics? Speed? Airbags? Safety restraints? Parts of body impacted?
    • System Considerations:
      • Hollow organs vs. solid. (i.e. liver vs. spleen)
      • Contusions to respiratory or cardiovascular structures?
      • Broken bones? blood loss?

  • Environmental: Bites and Stings
    • Differential rule outs: type of stinger/bite, underlying conditions
    • System Considerations:
      • Nervous: seizures
      • Cardiovascular: dangerous rhythms, bleeding disorders
      • Respiratory: asthma
      • Immunosupressive: anaphylaxis, swelling

  • Environmental: Drowning
    • Special considerations: temperature of water. Time submerged.
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: dangerous cardiac rhythms
      • Nervous: seizures
      • Musculoskeletal: bleeding, trauma sustained
      • Respiratory: airway patency

  • Environmental: High Altitude Illness
    • Differential rule outs: CVA, diabetic, respiratory illnesses, heart attack
    • System Considerations:
      • Nervous: seizures, CVA
      • Cardiovascular: CVA, heart attack
      • Respiratory: COPD, asthma
      • Endocrine: blood sugar

  • Environmental: Hyperthermia
    • Differential rule outs: altered mental status, drugs, alcohol, seizures, CHF, heart attack
    • System Considerations:
      • Respiratory: pulmonary edema
      • Nervous: seizures, shivering, drugs/alcohol
      • Cardiovascular: CHF
      • Endocrine: diabetes
      • Musculoskeletal: cramps

  • Environmental: Hypothermia
    • Differential rule outs: drugs/alcohol, seizures, diabetes, cardiac arrest
    • System Considerations:
      • Cardiovascular: cardiac arrest
      • Endocrine: diabetes

  • OB/GYN
    • Childbirth
      • Considerations: previous pregnancies and history, duration and frequency of contractions, crowning, presentation, meconium staining, prenatal care, known complications, current trimester, drug/alcohol/tobacco
    • Childbirth complications
      • Cord prolapse: place mother in knee chest position, push infant’s head up and off of the cord.
      • Placenta privia: placenta develops over the cervical wall and bleeds heavily when dilation occurs. Place mother in knee/chest position and urge not to bear down.
      • Abruptio placenta: placenta torn off of the uterine wall, causing catastrophic bleeding. Treat patient for shock.
      • Nuchal cord: cord wrapped around baby’s head. Slip cord off, or clamp cord and cut.
      • Breech presentation: place the mother in the knee/chest position or on her left side with hips elevated and urge her not to bear down.
    • Eclampsia
      • Presence of seizures or not?
      • Blood pressure
    • Trauma
      • Remember that mother can be fine, while the baby is in distress
      • Mother can compensate longer before showing signs of distress
    • Vaginal bleeding
      • Heavy or light?
      • Associated abdominal pain?
      • Prenatal care?
