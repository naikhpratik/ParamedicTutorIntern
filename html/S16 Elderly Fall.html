16. Elderly Fall 2

You are called emergent to a 92 year old male patient who fell in his foyer. Patient states he was changing the coolant in a neighbor’s car and went back into the house and slipped. Patient denies hitting head or neck, denies head or neck pain on palp. Patient’s right leg appears shortened and externally rotated. Patient has some movement of right leg but greatly diminished. Patient doesn’t complain of pain and asks you to pick him up off the floor.
  • What should you do for this patient?

    1. Move patient onto long spine board for transport to ED.
    2. Splint patient’s hip with sheet wrap, start IV, prepare for pain control if needed, CMS before and after splinting.
    3. Check CMS and lift patient off floor.
    4. Have patient sign refusal form then lift patient from ground.

  • The correct answer is 2. Splint patient’s hip with sheet wrap, start IV, prepare for pain control if needed, CMS before and after splinting.

    This patient most likely has a broken hip and should be taken to the ED for an X-ray. Although patient doesn't have much pain at this moment, he will likely have pain when moved from current position.

  • You start an IV and administer 50 mcg’s fentanyl before you start to move the patient. Patient then complains of nausea and starts to vomit. What should you do for this patient?

    1. Give narcan to reverse nausea
    2. Administer anti-emetics per protocol
    3. Give Benadryl as an antiemetic
    4. Transport to nearest facility and report possible overdose of fentanyl

  • The correct answer is 2. Administer anti-emetics per protocol.

    This is a common side effect of narcotic medication administration. The rate of administration and patient's health profile will have an effect on how patient reacts to pain control.
