26. Crash with Cops

You are called emergent to a 36 year old male patient who was involved in a single car MVA. Officer’s state that patient is non compliant and you find him face down in handcuffs. Patient has some small abrasions to his mouth from falling to the ground. Officer’s state that he didn’t put his hands up, didn’t follow commands and is probably drunk.
  • Patient presents with confused speech and tachypnea. Patient does not make threats and has no weapons. Patient has strong radial pulse. What would you like to do first for this patient?

    1. Keep patient restrained until further assessment is obtained
    2. Chemically sedate patient with Haldol
    3. Take spinal precautions and remove handcuffs
    4. Remove handcuffs

  • The correct answer is 3. Take spinal precautions and remove handcuffs.

    This patient is confused and not currently posing a threat. Although the police have placed him in handcuffs, it will be important to get them removed for assessment. C-spine injury cannot be ruled out and precautions should be taken.

You move to the back of the ambulance, start an IV and take a set of vital signs. Patient is confused but able to respond to most questions and you notice a diabetic bracelet on patient’s right wrist. You check the patient's sugar and the glucometer reads “HIGH.” You check the sugar again and the glucometer still says “HIGH.”
  • Based on this information, what is most likely making the patient altered?

    1. DKA
    2. Hypoglycemia
    3. ETOH
    4. Kidney failure

  • The correct answer is 1. DKA.

    This patient is suffering from DKA which is a buildup of ketones from a lack of insulin in the body. This patient will be treated in the ER with insulin. You can help the patient initially by giving a fluid bolus to dilute some of the sugar buildup.
