20. Unknown Medical Issue

You are called emergent to an unknown medical problem. The family on scene speaks mostly Spanish and points to the couch where the patient is sitting. Patient is a 20’s male who has a wrap around back brace on; it is apparent patient recently had surgery. Patient looks very sick, is currently grey and skin is sweaty. Patient makes groaning noises when questioned. You check BGL at 130 and someone on scene asks if patient still has pulse. You feel for a pulse and are not sure if the patient has a pulse or not, but patient appears close to death and pulseless.
  • What should you do next?

    1. Move patient to floor and start CPR
    2. Place patient on 4 lead cardiac monitor
    3. Place patient on non-rebreather mask
    4. Start an IV

  • The correct answer is 1. Move patient to floor and start CPR.

    This patient is sick, pulse is unknown, and CPR should be started.

You start CPR and patient becomes more alert. Patient states, “What is happening to me?” You ask patient if he is in pain and there is no more response. You are very confused and place the patient on the monitor where you see this strip:

    • You attempt to get a set of vital signs and are unable to get an accurate set of vitals. Patient’s heart rate then goes down to 20 bpm. No pulse is felt.

      What is your best option at this time?

      1. Start an IV
      2. Resume CPR
      3. Attempt cardioversion
      4. Attempt to place an OPA

    • The correct answer is 2. Resume CPR.

      Patient doesn’t have a pulse, so CPR is indicated.

    • Patient has no response to CPR this time, you start an IV, and intubate this patient. You look for a line of demarcation on the patient’s chest and believe that you see one.

      At this time, it would be best to:

      1. Give epinephrine 1:10 or other drugs per ACLS
      2. Give a fluid bolus of 20cc’s/kg
      3. Check patient’s pupils and if unequal, give lidocaine before intubation
      4. Rapid transport to the hospital

    • The correct answer is 4. Rapid transport to the hospital.

      This patient is suffering from a PE and the resulting obstructive shock. The line of demarcation and strange presentation are indicators. Rapid transport to the ED is needed. ACLS drugs are required, but will no save this patient’s life since he is suffering an obstruction.
