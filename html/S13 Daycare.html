13. Daycare

You are called emergent to a local daycare where a 10 year old child has fallen and hurt his wrist. Patient is alert and oriented on arrival and holding wrist. Patient’s wrist has obvious deformity and an “S” shape to the wrist. Patient is crying in pain and saying “help,” “help,” “help.” Staff deny loss of consciousness and state that patient fell off slide and onto wrist. Staff deny patient hitting head or neck. It is 11:00 on a Monday and there are many children and staff around.
  • What is the best initial treatment option for this child?

    1. Focused trauma assessment, splinting and transport
    2. Focused trauma assessment, IV, pain meds
    3. Focused trauma assessment, splint wrist, contact parents for consent and care
    4. Wait for parents before assessing patient

  • The correct answer is 3. Focused trauma assessment, splint wrist, contact parents for consent and care.

    Although it would be nice to do more for this child, this is not a life threat and implied consent is not valid here. Parents must be contacted before invasive treatment can begin.
