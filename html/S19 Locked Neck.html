19. Locked Neck

You are called emergent to a county mental health building where a patient is acting strange. The staff meet you at the door and report that the patient has been unable to move their neck for some time, and it seems to be "locked." You assess the patient and he is a 30's male currently AAOx4 and complaining of some neck pain, but not severe. Patient states that he has never had this happen to him before and he needs help. Patient’s head is currently turned to the right and he unable to look up or down. Patient can move eyes like normal. Patient reports that he can hear like normal, patient reports no weakness on left or right side. You ask to see the medication list. The patient's meds have recently changed and he reports Thorazine, Tegretol, Xanax, Haldol and Lithium as needed.

  • What are these type of neck/face spasms called?

    1. Stroke
    2. Bells Palsy
    3. Torticollis
    4. Ocular motor reaction

  • The correct answer is 3. Torticollis.

    Torticollis or Dystonic reaction are the terms for this type of spasm.

  • What medication should you give to alleviate these spasms?

    1. Valium
    2. Fentanyl
    3. Narcan
    4. Benadryl

  • The correct answer is 4. Benadryl.

    Benadryl is used to treat these spasms. Benzodiazepines can make the spasms worse, and the benzo’s will do nothing to “relax” the muscles in this case.

  • After administering Benadryl, how long will it take for the medication to take effect?

    1. Minutes
    2. Hours
    3. It is best to wait as long as it takes
    4. It varies by patient

  • The correct answer is 1. Minutes.

    Yes, it will vary by patient, but it usually takes only seconds to minutes.
