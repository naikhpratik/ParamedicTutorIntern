18. Elderly Woman Fall

You are called emergent to a 90 year old female for a stroke. Patient lives alone at an apartment, but patient has caregivers come and assist patient daily. Caregiver called because patient was found on the kitchen floor in a pool of blood with about 500cc’s of blood on the floor. Fall was unwitnessed at an unknown time. Patient is responsive to pain only with slow respirations < 6 minute. Caregiver on scene states patient has a DNR but no one is able to find DNR during patient assessment.
  • What should you do first for this lady?

    1. Preform head tilt chin lift and place patient on NRB mask
    2. Assist ventilation with jaw thrust, BVM and OPA
    3. Intubate patient
    4. Immobilize patient to long spine board

  • The correct answer is 2. Assist ventilation with jaw thrust, BVM and OPA.

    This patient needs assistance breathing with BVM. Neck should be stabilized at the same time. True treatment plan will depend on resources on scene, but ventilation is key.

Patient doesn’t take OPA because her jaw is clenched. Patient has good compliance to BVM ventilations with no airway adjunct. Physical assessment is unremarkable aside from laceration on temple and pupils are constricted and equal. Vitals come back at pulse 80, BP 144/p, respirations 6 bpm, BGL 120, SPO2 90% on room air. All IV attempts are unsuccessful.
  • What else should be done for this patient?

    1. Call stroke alert at ED
    2. Consider narcan IN if patient doesn't improve with ventilation
    3. Consider other reasons for altered mention
    4. Transport to nearest facility

  • The correct answer is 2. Consider narcan IN if patient doesn't improve with ventilation.

    Despite this call coming in as a stroke, there is no evidence of stroke at this time. This patient took too many opioids and fell to the floor. Airway control is appropriate, but patient has slow respirations and pinpoint pupils so narcan is indicated.
