5. Back Pain

You are called emergent to a 49 year old male patient with back pain. Patient states that he was working in the yard today and moved a heavy concrete bench. Patient states he thought that he injured his back but the pain hasn’t gone away for several hours. Patient is AAOx4 denies cardiac or respiratory history. Patient states he took Ibuprofen without relief about an hour ago. Vitals are BP 142/66, HR 90, respirations 20 unlabored. You place the patient on a 4 lead and 12 lead and this is what you see:

    • What would you call this infarct pattern?

      1. Septal-anterior
      2. Anterior-lateral
      3. Inferior-posterior
      4. Inferior- lateral

    • The correct answer is 2. Anterior-lateral.

      Note the ST segment elevation in I and AVL (lateral) as well as V2-V6 (anterior and lateral).

    • Considering coronary artery anatomy, where do you think the blockage is located in the heart?

      1. Distal LAD
      2. Left circumflex
      3. At the bifurcation of LAD and left circumflex
      4. Must perform posterior leads to know for sure

    • The correct answer is 3. At the bifurcation of LAD and left circumflex.

      Because the injury is anterior and extends to the lateral wall, the best guess is at the bifurcation of the LAD and circumflex.

    • This patient states that he is allergic to aspirin and when he takes it his belly becomes upset. Patient denies history of GI bleed or ulcer. In this chest pain scenario, what is the best course of action for this patient regarding his aspirin allergy?

      1. Administer aspirin
      2. Aspirin is now contraindicated
      3. Aspirin should be withheld because of the risk to patient
      4. Give less than the normal dose of aspirin

    • The correct answer is 1. Administer aspirin.

      Aspirin is one of the few medications that has been linked to better patient outcomes in the setting of acute MI. A belly ache is not a contraindication, but if the patient states his neck swells up when he takes ASA, it’s contraindicated. Local protocol will dictate.
