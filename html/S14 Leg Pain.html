14. Leg Pain

You are called emergent to a 40 year old female patient who is complaining of left leg pain. Patient states that her pain is getting worse for the last few hours. Patient isnâ€™t sure why her leg is hurting, and states that this has never happened to her before. Patient states pain is 10/10 and nothing makes it better or worse. Patient is able to move her leg with some difficulty. Patient denies feeling weak on one side, syncope, and trauma. Patient states that she is a smoker and the only medication she takes is birth control.
  • What should you do for this patient?

    1. Physical assessment, pain control
    2. High flow O2, transport
    3. Physical assessment, transport
    4. Attempt to have patient sign refusal form

  • The correct answer is 1. Physical assessment, pain control.
  • Physical assessment reveals left leg is cool, pale, and tingling. CMS is intact distally but appears diminished. What is most likely causing this condition?

    1. Deep vein thrombosis
    2. Pulmonary embolus
    3. Arterial occlusion
    4. Air embolus

  • The correct answer is 3. Arterial occlusion.

    These are the signs of arterial occlusion, along with some risk factors. Despite the fact that CMS is intact, the leg is cool, pale, tingling and has developed the signs of arterial occlusion. If the leg was hot to the touch, then DVT would be suspected.
