8. Lightheaded

You are called emergent to a 55 year old male’s house because he is not feeling well. You arrive and the patient makes several comments about being embarrassed. The patient is trying to use the bathroom when you arrive. You walk over to patient and he appears to be sweating although it is not too hot in the house. You check vital signs at BP 98/60, R – 46, Respirations 16 unlabored, skin pink, warm, diaphoretic. Patient states that he is feeling lightheaded and needs help getting up. This is the EKG that you obtain:

    • What is the best name for this EKG rhythm?

      1. Sinus bradycardia
      2. Junctional escape
      3. IVR
      4. Accelerated junctional

    • The correct answer is 1. Sinus bradycardia.

      The patient might have an old inferior wall MI present as well based on pathologic Q wave formation in lead III.

    • In order for a Q wave to be considered abnormal or pathologic, it must be:

      1. Any Q wave can be considered abnormal on 12 lead EKG
      2. > 0.4 seconds wide or 1/3 R wave height
      3. > 0.8 seconds wide or 1/2 R wave height
      4. > 0.12 seconds wide and occurring in contiguous leads

    • The correct answer is 2. > 0.4 seconds wide or 1/3 R wave height.

      The rule for pathologic Q waves are > 0.4 secs (one box wide) or greater than ⅓ R wave height. Q waves indicate old MI’s, but Q waves are not a good indicator when there is a bundle branch block present.

    • What is the best treatment option for this patient at this time?

      1. High flow O2 and transport
      2. Pace
      3. Atropine
      4. Epinephrine

    • The correct answer is 3. Atropine.

      At this point the patient is symptomatic but still AAOx4. This patient should have vitals, 4 lead, 12 lead, IV, and atropine.

    • What is the ACLS recommended atropine dose for this patient?

      1. 0.5mg
      2. 0.5g
      3. 3mg
      4. 3g

    • The correct answer is 1. 0.5mg.

      The dose is 0.5 mg every 5 minutes up to 3mg.
