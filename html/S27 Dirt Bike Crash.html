27. Dirt Bike Crash

You are called emergent to a house. The patient states that he was riding his dirt bike and crashed about 30 minutes prior. Patient states that he was able to ride and load his bike, but only with extreme pain. Patient called 911 because he hasn’t been able to catch his breath and he feels like it is getting worse. Patient now talks in 3-4 word sentences and is having a hard time breathing. Physical assessment reveals that most of patient’s left side is covered in blood and road rash. Left shoulder appears dislocated. Left collarbone in broken. Patient has vitals of BP 90/70, HR 136, Skin is diaphoretic, SPO2 89%, JVD present, breath sounds diminished on the left but difficult to be sure because patient can not take a deep breath. Patient is in a tripod position. Patient thinks he has broken ribs and screams in pain if they are touched.
  • What should you do first for this patient?

    1. Place patient on backboard
    2. Give patient Morphine for control pain
    3. Preform needle decompression of left chest
    4. Give patient a fluid bolus to see if pressure improves

  • The correct answer is 3. Preform needle decompression of left chest.

    This patient has the signs and symptoms of tension pneumothorax and needs a chest decompression.

  • Where is the best place to preform chest decompression on this patient?

    1. 2nd intercostal space midaxillary
    2. 2nd intercostal space midclavicular
    3. 3rd intercostal space midclavicular
    4. 5th intercostal space midaxillary

  • The correct answer is 2. 2nd intercostal space midclavicular.

    The 2nd intercostal space midclavicular is the best place for the inital chest decompression attempt. 5th intercostal space midaxillary is the second choice.
