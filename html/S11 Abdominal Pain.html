11. Abdominal Pain

You are called emergent to a 45 year old male patient who has abdominal pain. The patient was watching daytime television and suddenly started to have abdominal pain. Patient states pain is 10/10 and this has never happened to him before. Patient isn’t sure what caused the pain and denies recent illness or trauma. Patient states pain doesn’t radiate anywhere. Patient thought using the bathroom would make the pain better but there has been no change to pain level. Patient points to upper left and right upper quadrant when asked to locate pain. Patient denies medications, pertinent history, and allergies. Physical assessment is unremarkable and patient guards abdomen x 4 quadrants.
  • What is the best treatment plan for this patient?

    1. Vitals, IV, 12 lead, pain medications
    2. Vitals, IV, pain control
    3. Vitals, high flow O2, 12 lead, ASA, and nitro if needed
    4. Vitals, transport to nearest facility for suspected AAA

  • The correct answer is 1. Vitals, IV, 12 lead, pain medications.

    This patient should get an IV and pain meds, but pointing to left and right upper quadrants would make me want to perform a 12 lead looking for an acute MI.

The vitals come back pulse 140, respirations 24, BP 160/100, SPO2 100%. You start an IV and give this patient 5mg morphine. Patient states pain is now 9/10 and his left shoulder is starting to hurt. You get the 12 lead back and this is the result:

    • Based on this 12 lead, what should you do for this patient now?

      1. Give 324 ASA, and nitro every 5 minutes if pressure is > 100mmHg, and transport
      2. Relay to the hospital that this patient is suspected of having an MI
      3. Relay to hospital that patient is in SVT and give 6mg adenosine
      4. Consider other causes for abdominal pain, offer more pain control, and transport

    • The correct answer is 4. Consider other causes for abdominal pain, offer more pain control, and transport.

      This EKG is atrial tach. There is no reason to start down the chest pain algorithm. This patient exhibited classic Kehr’s sign. Abdominal pain with guarding and left shoulder pain. This patient had a perforation in the stomach, or intestine and contents were leaking out.
