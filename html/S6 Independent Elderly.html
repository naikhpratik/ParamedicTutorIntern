6. Independent Elderly

Your patient is an 82 year old male with altered mentation. Patient is aroused only to sternal rub. Patient lives at home and is normally very independent. Son was called to the residence when his father called and said he isn’t feeling well. Son states that patient has history of heart problems including CABG and defibrillation. Son hands you a list of meds that include Levothyroxine, Senna, Atenolol, Digoxin, Xeralto, Coumadin and Paxil. You take vitals as BGL 200, BP 80/p, R 160, respirations 12 unlabored. This is the EKG that you see:

    • What would you call this strip?

      1. Supraventricular tachycardia
      2. WPW
      3. Atrial tachycardia
      4. Uncontrolled A-fib with rapid ventricular response

    • The correct answer is 4. Uncontrolled A-fib with rapid ventricular response.

      This strip initially looks regular but close examination will reveal that some beats come slightly sooner or later than others especially between beats 5-6. This is uncontrolled A-fib with rapid ventricular response (RVR).

    • What do you think is causing this patient’s altered mentation?

      1. Patient has lost atrial kick
      2. Possible stroke
      3. Heart rate is too fast for ventricular filling
      4. New onset A-fib

    • The correct answer is 3. Heart rate is too fast for ventricular filling.

      Not only is the heart rate is not allowing for ventricular filling, but based on the medication list, this patient also has a previous history of A-fib. A-fib is not a new condition for this patient.

    • What is the risk associated with synchronized cardioversion for this patient?

      1. There is a risk that a blood clot will become dislodged from the atria and will cause a stroke.
      2. There is a risk that this patient is having a stroke and this heart rate is in response to increased ICP.
      3. There is no significant risk for this patient if he has been taking Coumadin.
      4. There is a risk that the patient will die because cardioversion can eliminate their heart rate.

    • The correct answer is 1. There is a risk that a blood clot will become dislodged from the atria and will cause a stroke.

      Based on the medication list, blood thinners and Digoxin, this patient has a previous history of A-fib. If the patient is not properly anti-coagulated there is a high risk of stroke with cardioversion.

    • Out of the list below, what is the best way to control this patient’s heart rate?

      1. Adenosine
      2. Atropine
      3. Beta blockers
      4. Synchronized cardioversion

    • The correct answer is 3. Beta blockers.

      Synchronized cardioversion is risky before measuring patient’s INR, or coagulation level. Cardioversion can cause a stroke.
