17. Altered Mental Status

You are called emergent to a 30 year old male with altered mentation. Patient is in neat, clean house and family state that he just returned from a house party, but was fine several minutes prior. Patient now responds to painful stimuli only. Family denies medications, pertinent history, and allergies.
  • In addition to checking vitals, what check is important for this patient?

    1. Check BGL
    2. Cincinnati stroke scale
    3. Try an NPA, then intubate patient if needed
    4. Check for asthma attack with ETCO2

  • The correct answer is 1. Check BGL.

    In this case, it is appropriate to check BGL and get a set of vitals before moving much further along. You wouldn’t want to intubate a diabetic.

  • Vitals come back, BP 110/82, pulse rate of 84, respirations 6 unlabored, pupils 2 mm’s, BGL 156, SPO2 94% on room air. Physical assessment is unremarkable. What should you do for this patient after getting vital signs?

    1. Take spinal precautions since patient might have fallen
    2. Assist ventilation with BVM
    3. Intubate this patient
    4. Place patinet on NRB mask

  • The correct answer is 2. Assist ventilation with BVM.

    The patient is only breathing 6/min. This patient will need assistance with BVM despite the fact that SPO2 is currently okay.

  • What should you do next for this patient?

    1. Transport to nearest facility
    2. Place patient on non-rebreather mask
    3. Administer 25 g D50
    4. Administer narcan

  • The correct answer is 4. Administer narcan.

    The patient has altered mental status of unknown cause. Depressed respirations with small pupils makes you suspect opioid overdose. Patient is responding to pain only and has a poor respiratory drive. It could be ETOH toxicity causing these symptoms, but narcan should be attempted in this scenario.
